About us

We Supply Burmese Natural Untreated Rubies & Sapphires

Jewelry Management was founded in 2014 as a merger between two families whose love for collecting jewelry helped tie the knot. For the past 30 years, both families have been avid collectors and dealers of the finest and most valuable gems in the world.

Today, Jewelry Management stands on the simple principle of quality first. Over the years, our quality first concept has helped us cut, polish, set, and sold many important pieces of jewelry to prominent Asian collectors including many pieces who have ended up in international auctions such as Sotheby’s and Christies.

We know that high end collectors care most about each gem’s real value, and whether each gem’s value will be able to stand the test of time and appreciate. Because only gems of the highest quality will be able to stand the test of time and appreciate,Gems chosen under our strict quality first guidelines will never have a problem finding a home.

At Jewelry Management, our mission is to inspire more people to collect jewelry. We aim to provide the knowledge and confidence collectors desire to begin collecting the finest gems in the world.

Gemstones

From Myanmar (Burma)

For many centuriessome of the most important colored gems in the world have been mined in Burma (Myanmar). Currently the most prestigious origin for Rubies, Sapphires and Jadeite, the land also produces other high quality gems such as Spinel, Peridot, Tourmaline, Moonstone and more than 50 other gemstones.

Most rubies and sapphires (around 85%) on the market have been through a process Heat Treatment or Clarity Enchancement, which improves a stone’s presentation from color to clarity. By treating the gem with high heat, one of the major components in a gemstones growth cycle, the clarity inclusions within the gem will begin to heal and the colors will become more pronounced.

However, at JM, we believe that heat treated robs a gem of its natural value. Therefore, we insist on selecting only the finest natural non heated gems. Only the best of the best untreated gemstones will stand the test of history and increase in value over time.

Ruby

The most important origin

The most important origin of Ruby

Burmese Rubies are famous worldwide for their exceptional coloring, deep blood red with a slightly bluish hue, which also known as “Pigeon Blood” red. While most rubies on the market from Africa or Thailand are brownish and dark, the high fluorescence and intense red of Burmese rubies really stands out. However, a Burmese ruby rarely grows pasta few carats, which makes a larger piece of ruby from 4 carats up extra valuable and high quality Burmese ruby over 5 carats nearly extinct in the market.

Sapphire

The most noble color

The noble color – Royal Blue Sapphire

Another famous member from the corundum family will be sapphire. Since the most famous silky cornflower blue sapphires from Kashmir have becomelegend, theRoyal Blue color from Burma has now become a new favoritefor sapphire collectors. Sapphires from Burma possess a rich and intense hue that stones from other sources even Kashmir can hardly have, which makes Sapphires from Burma extraordinary and unique.
